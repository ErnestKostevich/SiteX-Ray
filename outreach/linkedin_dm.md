# LinkedIn cold-DM playbook

The goal: send 50 personalized DMs per day to US/UK/CA/AU SMB owners, get 1–3 to reply, convert 1 to a $39 sale.

The hook in every DM: a **specific, real observation** about their website (not generic flattery). To generate the observation, run their URL through your free teaser tool first, then mention the most striking finding.

---

## Pre-flight (do once)

- Optimize your LinkedIn profile: clear headline ("Helping SMB owners fix invisible website issues — free 60-second audit"), simple photo, link to your landing in the Featured section.
- Connect with relevant people: search "owner [niche] [city]" and connect with 20–30/day. Wait 1–2 days, then DM connected people.
- Don't pitch on connect. Connect first, DM second. Higher reply rates.

---

## Variant A — opener with specific observation (highest conversion)

> Hey [First name] — I run a tool that audits small-business websites in 60 seconds, and I ran yours just to see. Quick thing: your homepage title is "[their actual title]" — generic titles like that typically lose 15–25% of search clicks vs a specific one. I wrote up the full report (5 dimensions, top fixes). Want me to send it over, on the house?

**When to use:** all cold prospects on your initial list.
**Why it works:** you've done the work first, you're not asking — you're offering. The specific quote of their title proves you actually looked.

---

## Variant B — opener with category-specific pain

> Hey [First name] — saw you run [their business]. Quick question: when someone Googles "[their primary keyword]" do you show up on page 1? I built an AI audit tool, ran yours, and found 3 specific things keeping you off the front page. Want me to send the report? Free.

**When to use:** when you don't have their site yet, just their LinkedIn.
**Why it works:** Page-1 visibility is a top SMB anxiety. The "3 specific things" promise teases value.

---

## Variant C — opener with a missing-thing observation

> Hey [First name] — quick FYI, I noticed [their site] doesn't have a phone number above the fold. For [niche] sites that typically cuts call-volume by 30–40%. I have a full 60-second audit report I can email you if useful — no signup, no upsell.

**When to use:** when you spot one OBVIOUS critical issue (no CTA, no meta description, no contact info, page-not-mobile-friendly).
**Why it works:** Names a specific, easy-to-verify problem with a measurable consequence.

---

## Follow-up #1 (3 days after no reply)

> Hey [First name] — circling back. The audit's a 5-min read and points to 3 things that take ~30 min to fix. Total free. Just need an email to send it to. Worth a look?

---

## Follow-up #2 (7 days after no reply, then stop)

> [First name] — last note from me. If audits aren't useful right now, totally fine — but here's the headline finding from your site in case it's useful: [paste the single biggest issue from the teaser]. Good luck, [your name].

**Why this works as a closer:** even if they don't reply, they got real value and remember you. Some come back months later.

---

## When they reply asking for the report

> Awesome — what's the best email to send it to? I'll have it in your inbox within 5 minutes.

After sending the free teaser (the auto-generated one from your tool), follow up 24 hours later:

> Hey [First name] — did the teaser report land ok? The full version has the deep dive on all 5 dimensions and the 90-day plan — $39 if you want the rest. Otherwise no pressure, glad the teaser helps.

---

## When they're hostile / skeptical

**"Is this an AI thing?"** → "Yep — Claude AI generates the analysis, but the checklist is from real conversion audits. I read every report before sending. Want me to send yours?"

**"How much does it cost?"** → "Teaser is free, no strings. Full report is $39 one-time. Done-for-you (we implement the fixes) starts at $299."

**"I already have an SEO guy."** → "Awesome — even better, your SEO person will tell you in 5 min whether the audit's worth using. Send to me and I'll run it free."

**"Send me the link."** → Send your landing page URL. Don't argue.

---

## Anti-patterns (don't do)

- ❌ "I hope you're doing well" / "Hope this email finds you well" — instant delete
- ❌ "We're a leading AI auditing platform" — they don't care
- ❌ Generic "I checked out your site and have some ideas" — sounds like every other pitch
- ❌ Long DMs (> 6 lines) — won't be read
- ❌ Attachments or PDFs — flagged as spam by LinkedIn
- ❌ Sending the actual report in a DM — too long, won't read; always email it

## Daily routine (1.5 hours)

| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:30 | Find 30–50 prospects (Sales Nav alternatives, see `niche_lists.md`) |
| 0:30–1:00 | Run each through your free-teaser tool, grab a striking finding per person |
| 1:00–1:30 | Send 30–50 personalized DMs using one of the variants above |
| End of day | Reply to anyone who responded; queue tomorrow's follow-ups |

Track in a spreadsheet: name, company, URL, DM date, replied?, sale?
