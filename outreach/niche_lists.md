# Where to find prospects (free + paid)

You want **English-speaking SMB owners in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and Western EU**, in niches where the site directly drives revenue. The sweet spot:

- Owner is busy and not technical
- Site is 2+ years old, probably built by an agency they don't talk to anymore
- Each customer is worth $100+ (so a 10% conversion lift means real money)
- Local services tend to convert best — the website is the storefront

---

## Tier-1 niches (highest conversion based on similar tools)

| Niche | Why they convert | Where to find them |
|---|---|---|
| **Dentists, orthodontists, dermatologists** | High LTV ($500–5000 per patient), local search-dependent | Yelp Health categories, Google Maps, ADA directory |
| **Lawyers — family, personal injury, immigration** | Single client = $5k–50k. Site is half their marketing. | Avvo.com, state bar directories, lawyers.justia.com |
| **Plumbers, electricians, HVAC, roofers** | Emergency searches = high commercial intent | Yelp Home Services, Angi.com, BBB directories |
| **Real-estate brokers + property managers** | Individual agent sites are everywhere, mostly bad | Realtor.com, Zillow agent pages |
| **Independent restaurants** (only with online ordering) | If they're losing reservations to a bad UX, they care | OpenTable, Yelp, Google Maps |
| **Boutique fitness — yoga, pilates, CrossFit** | Brand-conscious, will pay for polish | ClassPass directory, MindBody-listed studios |
| **B2B SaaS pre-Series A** | Founders are themselves the marketers, very analytical | Crunchbase (free tier), Indie Hackers, Y Combinator alumni list |
| **E-commerce stores < $100k/yr** | Conversion = direct revenue, easy to demonstrate ROI | Shopify storefronts that show up for niche product searches |

## Where NOT to go

- ❌ Fortune 500 / enterprise — you'll wait months, lose to incumbent vendors
- ❌ Cryptocurrency / Web3 — high refund rate, sketchy ICP
- ❌ Adult industry — payment processors disallow
- ❌ Healthcare with HIPAA exposure (we don't process PHI but optics matter)
- ❌ RU / CIS / India — low ARPU + payment friction + per our targeting
- ❌ Anyone with < 1 year in business — no budget, no traction

## Free prospecting sources (no paid tools)

### LinkedIn (organic search)
1. LinkedIn → search "Owner [niche] [city]" — example: `Owner Dentist Boston`
2. Filter: 2nd-degree connections (lets you do a connect-then-DM flow)
3. For each profile: open their company website (usually in "Featured" or "About"), grab the URL, run through your audit tool, find the hook, DM
4. **Daily cap:** LinkedIn limits ~100 invites/week to avoid jail. Stay at ~20 connects/day to be safe.

### Google Maps (best for local services)
1. Search "[niche] near [city]" — e.g. `dentist near Boston`
2. Each result has a website link — that's your prospect
3. Owner names are usually on the About page or LinkedIn
4. Free, manual, ~30 prospects/hour

### Yelp Pro directories (US local services)
- yelp.com/c/boston/dentists, yelp.com/c/london/plumbers — each has owner-claimed pages with site links

### Industry directories (high-trust niches)
- **Lawyers:** avvo.com, lawyers.com, martindale.com
- **Dentists:** ada.org/find-a-dentist (US), gdc-uk.org (UK)
- **Real-estate:** realtor.com, zillow.com/professionals
- **Plumbers/Trades:** angi.com, homeadvisor.com (US), checkatrade.com (UK)

### Indie Hackers / Y Combinator alumni
- For B2B SaaS prospects: indiehackers.com → revenue board → reach out to founders < $10k MRR (they're in scrappy mode and will read your email)

## How to qualify a prospect in 30 seconds

Open their site. Quick checklist:

| Check | Pass? |
|---|---|
| Site loads in <5s | If not, they probably know it's slow and don't care → skip |
| Has a phone number somewhere | If totally none → too small / not a real business |
| At least 3–4 pages, not just a one-pager | Otherwise no audit material |
| Last blog/news post within 2 years | If 2017 was their last update → owner has checked out |
| English content | Otherwise outside our market |
| Real business (LLC, address, photos of actual people) | Avoid drop-shipping ghost stores |

Pass all 6 → run audit, send DM. Fail 2+ → next prospect.

## Daily volume math

To hit 5 sales / week (= $195 / week = ~$10k/year run rate):
- Need ~50 free teasers delivered per week
- Convert teaser → paid at ~10%
- Need ~500 reply-able prospects per week (10% reply rate)
- = 50 prospects × 5 days = **50 prospects/day**

At 30 sec to qualify + audit + DM each → **~25 min/day** of pure prospecting. Plus 30 min of follow-up + replies. Total: ~1 hour/day to hit the goal.

This scales linearly. Want $40k/year run rate? Hire a VA for $5/hour to do the prospect-list-building, you handle DMs and replies.
